Emergency numbers

Service Number Notes
Emergency (police, fire, ambulance) 119 English operators available
Police 112 English service available
Tourist helpline 1330 24/7, multilingual tourism support
Medical emergency info 1339 Medical advice line

Pro tip

Save 1330 in your phone. It's the Korea Travel Helpline — they can help with translation, directions, and tourist emergencies in English, Japanese, and Chinese.

If you get sick

  • Minor issues: Visit a pharmacy (약국) — pharmacists can recommend OTC medication. Use Papago to describe symptoms.
  • Hospital visit: Go to a large hospital (병원) emergency room for serious issues. International clinics in Itaewon and Gangnam have English-speaking staff.
  • Travel insurance: Call your insurance hotline before major treatment. Keep all receipts for claims.

If you lose something

  • On transit: Call Lost & Found at 120 (Seoul) or ask at any subway service center.
  • Taxi: Call 120 or use the receipt to contact the taxi company.
  • Passport: Report to local police (112), then contact your embassy immediately.

Watch out

Keep your passport with you or in a hotel safe. You can be fined for not carrying ID, though this is rarely enforced for tourists.

Embassy contacts

Look up your country's embassy in Seoul before traveling. Most are located in the Jongno or Yongsan districts. Register with your embassy's travel notification system if available.
