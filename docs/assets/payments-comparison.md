# Payments Comparison Infographic

Asset: `images/infographics/payments-comparison.svg`

Date: 2026-07-01

## Purpose

This infographic provides a simple visual comparison of common payment methods in Korea for foreign travelers.

It helps users understand the practical role of:

- Foreign cards
- Cash
- T-money
- WOWPASS

The asset is intended to support decision-making, not to replace detailed page content.

## Where to Use

Use this asset near content that explains:

- How to pay in Korea
- Which payment method to use by situation
- Foreign card limitations
- T-money and WOWPASS differences
- Why travelers should keep a backup payment method

Do not insert this SVG as a replacement for visible HTML comparison text. Important labels and explanations should also remain as page text for accessibility, translation, and SEO.

## Design Rules

- SVG vector only.
- No embedded raster images.
- No photos.
- No gradients.
- Simple line icons only.
- White background with a soft framed area.
- Rounded rectangles use `12px` radius.
- All labels remain editable SVG `<text>`.
- Keep spacing consistent across the four cards.
- Keep the design neutral and suitable for a business/travel guide page.

## Color Palette

- Navy primary: `#0F2747`
- Teal accent: `#16B6B0`
- Light border: `#DDE6EE`
- Soft background: `#F7FAFC`
- White background: `#FFFFFF`
- Secondary body text: `#52677A`

## Text Labels

Infographic title:

- `How to Pay in Korea`

Cards:

- `Foreign Card`
  - Best for: `Hotels, chains, restaurants`
  - Limits: `May fail online or at small shops`
- `Cash`
  - Best for: `Markets, small vendors, backup`
  - Limits: `Less convenient for daily use`
- `T-money`
  - Best for: `Subway, bus, some taxis`
  - Limits: `Not for general shopping`
- `WOWPASS`
  - Best for: `Payment, exchange, transport`
  - Limits: `Check fees and availability`

Footer note:

- `Use more than one payment method: card for convenience, cash for backup, T-money for transport.`
