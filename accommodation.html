Where to Stay in Seoul

Common mistakes

Avoid these Seoul hotel booking mistakes

Booking only by price.

Check transport, luggage route and area fit first.

Ignoring airport access.

Extra transfers and a difficult luggage route can drain the first day.

Ignoring luggage and hills.

Check the actual walking route, not only the map distance.

Choosing a nightlife area for a quiet stay.

Pick calmer areas if sleep is the priority.

Assuming subway distance means suitcase-friendly.

Station exits, stairs and elevators matter more than meters.

Booking far from the station.

Long walks feel longer with suitcases, rain or children.

Not checking late-night arrival options.

After midnight, train and bus choices can change.

FAQ

Where to stay in Seoul FAQ

What is the best area to stay in Seoul?

For most first-time visitors, Myeongdong is the easiest all-round base for sightseeing and shopping. Compare the alternatives in our where to stay in Seoul for first-time visitors guide.

Is Hongdae or Myeongdong better?

Choose Myeongdong for central sightseeing and shopping, or Hongdae for nightlife, cafés, and direct AREX access. Use the Hongdae vs Myeongdong comparison for the detailed trade-offs.

Which Seoul area is easiest with large luggage?

Seoul Station, Hongdae, and Mapo / Gongdeok are practical starting points, but the station exit, elevator, slope, and final walk still matter. The best Seoul areas for solo travelers guide compares several luggage-friendly bases.

Which area has the best airport access?

Seoul Station, Hongdae, and Mapo / Gongdeok have strong AREX access, while some Myeongdong hotels work well with airport buses. Compare the full arrival route in the Incheon Airport transfer guide.

Should I stay near Incheon Airport or in Seoul?

Stay near Incheon Airport for a very late arrival, very early departure, or short transit. For normal sightseeing days, staying in Seoul usually saves more time; check the arrival sequence in the Incheon Airport guide.

Which area is best for families?

Jamsil, Insadong, and Mapo / Gongdeok are useful family options when easier walking, food access, and calmer nights matter. Compare them in the guide to the best Seoul areas for families.

Which area is best for nightlife?

Hongdae, Itaewon, and parts of Gangnam are the main choices, but the best one depends on your preferred nightlife and tolerance for noise. Compare the best Seoul areas for nightlife.

Is Seoul Station a good place to stay?

Yes, especially for short stays, AREX access, KTX trips, or large luggage. It is practical rather than atmospheric; the best Seoul areas for solo travelers guide compares it with other convenient bases.

Is Gangnam too far for sightseeing?

Gangnam works when most plans are south of the river, but it is less convenient for palace areas, Myeongdong, and Hongdae. Compare central alternatives in the where to stay in Seoul for first-time visitors guide.

What should I check before booking a Seoul hotel?

Check the exact location, station exit, elevator access, slopes, airport route, check-in time, luggage storage, room size, noise reviews, and cancellation policy. If price is the main constraint, compare the best budget areas to stay in Seoul before choosing the cheapest room.
