Maps & Navigation

Google Maps is unreliable in Korea. Locals use Naver Map and KakaoMap — here's how to use them as a foreigner.

Why Google Maps doesn't work well

Google Maps has limited data in Korea due to national map data restrictions. Walking directions are often wrong, transit info is incomplete, and many businesses aren't listed. Don't rely on it as your primary navigation tool.

Watch out

Google Maps may show routes that don't exist or miss the best subway transfers. Always cross-check with a Korean map app.

Apps that actually work

App Best for English support
Naver Map Transit, walking, restaurants Good — switch language in settings
KakaoMap Transit, taxis, local search Good — English available
Citymapper Seoul subway navigation Full English
Google Maps General overview only Full English (limited data)

Naver Map — setup guide

  1. Download "Naver Map" from the App Store or Google Play.
  2. Open Settings → Language → select English.
  3. Search using English names — Naver recognizes most tourist spots.
  4. For addresses, paste the Korean address if you have it (from your hotel booking).
  5. Use the transit tab for subway/bus routes with real-time arrivals.

Pro tip

Save your hotel and key locations as favorites (star icon) on day one. You'll use them constantly.

KakaoMap — setup guide

  1. Download "KakaoMap" (not KakaoTalk — that's the messenger).
  2. Switch to English in app settings.
  3. Use it to call taxis via Kakao T integration.
  4. Restaurant reviews and photos are more detailed than Naver for dining.

Searching addresses in Korea

Korean addresses use a lot-based system that differs from Western street addresses. If you have trouble searching:

  • Search by business or building name instead of street address.
  • Use the Korean name from Naver or Google (even in Google, the Korean name helps).
  • Ask your hotel to write the address in Korean — show it to taxi drivers.
  • Drop a pin on the map and use "Directions to here."

Subway navigation tips

  • Subway apps show which car to board for the fastest exit transfer.
  • Last trains run around midnight — check times if you're out late.
  • Download offline subway maps as backup for underground dead zones.
  • Station names are announced in Korean, English, Japanese, and Chinese.

Offline backup

Download offline maps in Naver Map or KakaoMap before heading to areas with poor signal (mountains, rural areas). Screenshot your route and hotel address as a last resort.
