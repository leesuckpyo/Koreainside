Arriving at Incheon Airport

What to Do After Landing at Incheon Airport

Follow the arrival process in order, from checking your terminal and passing immigration to collecting baggage, clearing customs and choosing transport to your accommodation.

Choose the right route

Entering Korea or transferring?

Your next steps depend on whether you are entering Korea or connecting to another flight.

01 Entering Korea

Follow the arrival process below

Go through immigration, collect checked baggage, pass customs and enter the arrival hall. Before leaving, prepare mobile data and decide how you will reach your accommodation.

Immigration→Baggage Claim→Customs→Arrival Hall

02 Transferring to Another Flight

Follow your airline's transfer instructions

Follow Transfer or Connecting Flights signs instead of assuming that you should enter Korea. Confirm your connection time, terminal and whether your baggage is checked through with your airline.

Airport procedures can differ by itinerary, so use your airline's instructions and the airport's official transfer guidance.

Step by step

From the Aircraft to the Arrival Hall

Travelers entering Korea normally follow these five steps. Keep following the airport signs if your walking route looks different.

  1. 01

    Follow the Arrivals Signs

    After leaving the aircraft, follow signs for Arrivals or Immigration. Do not follow Transfer or Connecting Flights signs unless you are connecting to another flight.

  2. 02

    Immigration

    Keep your passport and any entry documents required for your travel status ready. Requirements depend on your nationality, visa status and travel circumstances.

    Check official entry guidance

    Korea Inside does not determine whether you are eligible to enter or which document you must submit. Check the official guidance before departure.

    K-ETA official guide ↗ External site · Opens in a new tab e-Arrival Card official website ↗ External site · Opens in a new tab
  3. 03

    Baggage Claim

    Check the airport screens for the baggage belt assigned to your flight. If your baggage does not arrive, contact your airline or the baggage service desk before leaving the baggage claim and customs area.

    Keep your baggage tag and flight details ready when asking for help.

  4. 04

    Customs

    Use the customs route that matches whether you have items to declare. If you are unsure about a declaration requirement, check the current official customs guidance.

  5. 05

    Arrival Hall

    Once you enter the public arrival hall, check your internet connection, accommodation address, payment method and transport plan before leaving the airport.

Know your terminal

Terminal 1 and Terminal 2 Are Separate Buildings

Confirm the terminal first, then use the map as an orientation aid for the arrival hall. Current flight information and airport signs take priority over fixed locations.

T1

Terminal 1

Terminal 1 is a separate building. Check your airline or the official airport arrival timetable before travel and follow the signs shown after landing.

T2

Terminal 2

Terminal 2 is not connected to Terminal 1 by a walking route. Use the airport's current inter-terminal transport guidance if you need to change terminals.

Use the map for orientation, not live operations.

It shows immigration, baggage claim, customs, arrival exits and common services such as SIM counters, currency exchange, rail, buses, taxis and rental cars. Facility positions and movement routes may change.

Before moving on

Before You Leave the Arrival Hall

Complete the checks that matter for your trip. Not every traveler needs the same SIM, transport card or payment product.

  • 01

    Connect to the internet

    Choose an eSIM, roaming or airport Wi-Fi based on what is already available to you.

  • 02

    Save your accommodation address

    Keep the English address and, if possible, the Korean address. Confirm the location in your map app.

  • 03

    Decide how you will pay

    Check whether you will use cash, an overseas payment card or another travel payment method. WOWPASS is optional, not a requirement for every traveler.

  • 04

    Choose your transport

    Compare AREX, airport bus, taxi and a pre-booked transfer using your destination, arrival time and luggage.

  • 05

    Buy a transport card only if needed

    T-money is useful if you plan to use public transport, but you do not need to treat it as an immediate purchase in every situation.

Quick answers

Incheon Airport Arrival FAQ

Short answers to the questions that can change what you do next.

How do I know whether my flight arrives at Terminal 1 or Terminal 2?

Check your airline booking or the official Incheon Airport arrival timetable using your flight number. It shows the terminal and may also show the arrival gate, baggage belt, arrival exit and current status.

Do I go through immigration before collecting my baggage?

Yes. Travelers entering Korea normally go through immigration first, then collect checked baggage, pass customs and enter the arrival hall. Transfer passengers should follow Transfer or Connecting Flights signs instead.

What should I do if my baggage does not arrive?

Check that you are waiting at the correct belt. If your baggage still does not arrive, contact your airline or the baggage service desk before leaving the baggage claim and customs area, and keep your baggage tag and flight details ready.

Can I walk between Terminal 1 and Terminal 2?

No. Terminal 1 and Terminal 2 are separate buildings and are not connected by a walking route. Confirm your terminal before travel and use the airport's official inter-terminal transport guidance if you need to move between them.

Which transport should I choose from Incheon Airport to Seoul?

Choose based on your destination, arrival time, luggage, group size and budget. AREX suits rail connections to Seoul Station or Hongdae; airport buses may reduce transfers; taxis and pre-booked transfers can be easier for late arrivals, groups or heavy luggage.

What should transfer passengers do?

Follow Transfer or Connecting Flights signs and your airline's instructions. Confirm your connection time, terminal and whether your baggage is checked through with your airline; do not follow the general immigration route unless instructed.
