Essential Apps

The apps locals actually use — for food, transport, translation, and daily life in Korea.

Must-have apps

Download these before or right after arriving. Most work with a foreign phone number for basic features.

Naver Map Navigation Reliable transit and walking directions

Food & ordering

Baemin (배달의민족)

Korea's largest food delivery platform. Browse menus with photos, order in-app, and track delivery in real time. English support is limited — use Papago to translate menus if needed.

  • Requires a Korean phone number for registration in most cases.
  • Payment via card or Kakao Pay.
  • Delivery fees are typically ₩2,000–4,000.

Coupang Eats

Coupang's delivery service with slightly better English support. Good alternative if Baemin registration fails. Coupang itself is Korea's Amazon — useful for fast delivery of everyday items.

Pro tip

At restaurants without English menus, use Papago's camera translation to scan the menu. It works remarkably well for Korean text.

Transport apps

  • Kakao T — Call taxis, see estimated fares, pay in-app. Works with foreign cards. Safer than hailing on the street.
  • Subway Korea — Offline subway map with route planner. Good backup for navigation.
  • Citymapper — Clean English interface for Seoul transit.

Communication

  • KakaoTalk — Korea's WhatsApp. Essential if you're meeting locals or joining groups. Free calls and messaging.
  • Papago — Translate text, voice, and images. Far better than Google Translate for Korean.

Shopping & convenience

  • Coupang — Everything delivered next-day (rocket delivery). Needs Korean phone number for full access.
  • Convenience store apps — CU, GS25 apps for pre-ordering items for pickup.

Watch out

Many Korean apps require phone number verification via SMS. If your foreign number doesn't receive Korean SMS, get a local SIM or eSIM with a Korean number first.
